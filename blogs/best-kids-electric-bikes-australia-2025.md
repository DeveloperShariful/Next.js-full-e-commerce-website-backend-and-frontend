## How to Choose the Best Kids Electric Bike (Buying Guide)

When shopping for an **Australian electric bike** for your child, keep these 4 factors in mind:

### 1. Size and Seat Height
The most common mistake parents make is buying a bike that is too big. Your child should be able to place both feet flat on the ground while seated.
*   **12-Inch Wheels:** Ages 3-5
*   **16-Inch Wheels:** Ages 5-8
*   **20-Inch+:** Ages 8+

### 2. Battery Safety
Look for lithium-ion batteries that are encased properly. At GoBike, all our batteries meet strict Australian safety standards. Removable batteries are a bonus so you can charge them inside without bringing the muddy bike in.

### 3. Speed Limiters
A good kids' e-bike must have parental controls. Look for bikes with "Low," "Med," and "High" speed settings. Start your child on Low until they master the brake.

### 4. Weight of the Bike
A lighter bike is safer. If a child falls, they should be able to pick the bike up themselves. Electric balance bikes are significantly lighter than traditional petrol dirt bikes.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Are electric bikes safe for 3-year-olds?**
Yes, specifically designed **electric balance bikes** are safe because they have low centers of gravity and slow speed modes. Always ensure they wear a helmet.

**How long does the battery last?**
Most high-quality kids' e-bikes last between 45 to 60 minutes of continuous riding, depending on the terrain and rider weight.

**Do I need a license for these bikes?**
In Australia, most kids' electric balance bikes are designed for private property use and do not require a license, but always check your local council laws regarding public paths.
