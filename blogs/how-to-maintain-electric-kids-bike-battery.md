## 1. The Science of Lithium-Ion: Why Care Matters

Before diving into the tips, it’s helpful to understand what’s happening inside your GoBike. Lithium-ion batteries are the industry standard for electric vehicles because they offer a high energy density without the heavy weight of old lead-acid batteries. 

However, Lithium-ion cells are sensitive to two main enemies: **extreme temperatures** and **deep discharging**. Every time a battery is drained to 0% and charged back to 100%, it completes a "cycle." Proper maintenance is all about reducing the stress on these cycles so the battery can last for years rather than months.

## 2. The Golden Rule of Charging: Don't Drain It Completely

One of the biggest myths in battery care is that you need to completely flatten the battery before recharging it. **This is false and highly damaging to modern e-bike batteries.**

Deep discharges put immense stress on lithium cells. If you constantly run your child's GoBike until the motor completely shuts off, you are actively destroying the battery's capacity. 
*   **The 20% Rule:** Try to recharge the bike when the battery level drops to around 20%. If you notice the bike struggling on small inclines or the power output dropping, it’s time to head back to the garage.
*   **Top-Up Charging is Great:** Lithium-ion batteries actually prefer "partial" charges. Topping up the battery after a 30-minute ride is perfectly fine and healthier than waiting for it to die.

## 3. The "Cool Down" Protocol (Crucial for Australian Summers)

This is perhaps the most important tip for Australian riders. When an electric bike is ridden—especially on thick grass or off-road trails—the battery generates heat.

**Never plug the charger into a hot battery.** 
Charging a battery while the internal cells are still hot from a ride accelerates chemical degradation and can be a severe safety hazard. 

*   **The 30-Minute Rule:** After your little ripper finishes their ride, let the bike sit in a shaded, cool area for at least 30 to 45 minutes before plugging it in. Let the battery return to room temperature first.

## 4. Smart Storage: Preparing for the Off-Season

There will be times when the bike isn't used for weeks or even months (like during a harsh winter or a long family holiday). How you store the bike during this downtime makes a massive difference.

*   **Never Store at 0% or 100%:** Leaving a battery completely empty for months can cause it to fall into a "deep sleep" from which it may never wake up. Conversely, storing it at 100% keeps the cells under high voltage stress.
*   **The Sweet Spot:** If you are putting the bike away for more than two weeks, charge the battery to about **50% to 60% capacity**.
*   **Climate Control:** The harsh Australian sun is a battery killer. Never leave the bike or the battery baking in a hot tin shed or the boot of your car. Store it indoors in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.

## 5. Riding Habits That Save Battery Life

Did you know that *how* your child rides affects how long the battery lasts on a single trip? While our GoBikes offer up to 60 minutes of run time, certain factors drain the battery faster.

*   **Tire Pressure:** Soft, under-inflated tires create rolling resistance. The motor has to work twice as hard to push the bike forward, draining the battery rapidly. Always ensure the tires are pumped up to the recommended PSI.
*   **Terrain Matters:** Riding on smooth pavement uses significantly less energy than riding through thick grass, mud, or up steep dirt hills.
*   **Use Speed Modes Wisely:** Our [GoBike electric motorbikes](/bikes) come with adjustable speed settings. Keeping the bike on the "Low" or "Eco" mode not only ensures safety for beginners but also dramatically extends the battery life per ride.

## 6. Water Safety: The "No Submarine" Policy

GoBikes are built tough for Aussie kids. They are water-resistant and can easily handle a splash from a shallow puddle or a light drizzle. However, they are **not waterproof**.

*   Never submerge the bike in water, ride through deep creeks, or leave it out in a heavy rainstorm. 
*   **Cleaning the Bike:** When it’s time to clean the mud off the bike, put down the high-pressure hose! High-pressure water can force its way into the battery compartment and electrical connectors. Instead, use a damp cloth, a soft brush, and mild soapy water to wipe down the frame.

## 7. Use Only Genuine GoBike Chargers

It might be tempting to use a generic charger you bought online because you misplaced the original one. **Don't do it.** 

Generic chargers often deliver the wrong voltage or lack the smart-cutoff technology that prevents overcharging. Using the wrong charger is the fastest way to fry your battery or, worse, cause a fire. If you’ve lost your charger, always order a genuine replacement.
