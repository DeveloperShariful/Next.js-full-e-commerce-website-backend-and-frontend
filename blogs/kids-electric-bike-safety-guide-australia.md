Safety is the #1 concern for any parent buying a motorized vehicle for their child. At GoBike, we design our bikes with safety first, but proper gear and supervision are equally important.

Here is the complete safety checklist for Australian parents.

## 1. The Helmet is Non-Negotiable
Never let your child ride without a helmet. For electric balance bikes, a standard bicycle helmet is okay for slow speeds, but for our "Pro Dirt" models, we recommend a **full-face helmet**.
*   **Why?** It protects the chin and teeth in case of a forward fall.
*   **Standard:** Look for the AS/NZS 2063:2008 standard sticker.

## 2. Speed Limiters are Your Best Friend
One of the best features of modern electric balance bikes (like the GoBike series) is the adjustable speed mode.
*   **Low Mode:** Usually caps at 8-10 km/h. Perfect for learning balance.
*   **High Mode:** Unlocks full potential only when you decide they are ready.

## 3. Elbow and Knee Pads
When learning to use a throttle, kids might tip over. Simple knee and elbow pads turn a scary fall into a "dust yourself off" moment.

## 4. Where to Ride?
*   **Safe Zones:** Flat grass, backyard, or empty parks.
*   **No-Go Zones:** Steep hills (for beginners), near traffic, or near swimming pools.

## 5. Pre-Ride Check
Teach your child to check these 3 things before every ride:
1.  **Brakes:** Squeeze the lever—does it stop the wheel?
2.  **Tires:** Are they firm?
3.  **Chain:** Is it looking normal (not hanging loose)?

Riding an electric bike is a skill that builds coordination. With the right gear, it is safer than a trampoline!
