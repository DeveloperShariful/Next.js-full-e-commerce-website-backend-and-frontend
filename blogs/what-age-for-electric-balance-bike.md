"Is my 3-year-old too young?" or "Is my 7-year-old too big?"
These are the most common questions we get at GoBike Australia.

The truth is, it's less about age and more about **inseam (leg length)** and **coordination**. However, here is a general guide to help you choose.

## Ages 2 to 4: The "Glider" Phase
*   **Recommended Bike:** 12-Inch Electric Balance Bike.
*   **How it works:** Start with the motor **OFF**. Let them use it as a push-bike. Once they can lift their feet and glide for 10 meters, they are ready for "Low Speed" mode.
*   **Goal:** Learning balance without training wheels.

## Ages 5 to 8: The "Rider" Phase
*   **Recommended Bike:** 16-Inch Electric Bike (Brushless Motor).
*   **Capabilities:** These kids are ready for more power. They can handle grass, dirt tracks, and small jumps.
*   **Key Feature:** Look for suspension models to smooth out the bumps.

## Ages 9 to 12+: The "Pro" Phase
*   **Recommended Bike:** High-Torque Dirt Versions / 24V or 36V systems.
*   **Capabilities:** Full off-road riding. These bikes bridge the gap between toys and real motorcycles.

## How to Measure Your Child
Have your child stand against a wall with shoes on. Measure from the crotch to the floor.
*   The bike's **seat height** should be equal to or slightly lower than this measurement so they can touch the ground flat-footed.
